Mode 2
Maturity Scan — D2
Score each indicator 1–5 based on direct observation. 1 = not present, 3 = partially embedded, 5 = fully embedded. Add notes for key findings.
Pillar 01
Advanced Manufacturing Technologies
Capability to deploy and operate production technologies that enhance process precision, flexibility, and efficiency, building on stable and standardised processes.
—
1.1
Are production technologies deployed on stable, standardised processes — or do they compensate for process instability?
1.2
How consistently are production technologies used across shifts and operators?
1.3
Is equipment capability matched to current product and process requirements?
1.4
Is there evidence that technology investments have improved consistency, throughput, or resource efficiency?
1.5
Is advanced manufacturing (additive, flexible automation, collaborative robotics) in use or actively planned?
Sustainability check
Pillar 02
Digital Factory
Capability to capture, access, and use operational data across the organisation, including in environments where data is distributed across fragmented or weakly integrated systems.
—
2.1
Can operational data (production, inventory, quality) be accessed and combined across functions without manual reconciliation?
2.2
Is there a single version of truth for key operational metrics across departments?
2.3
How current and reliable is operational data at the point of decision?
2.4
Are ERP, MES, or equivalent systems actively used by operations teams — not just accounting?
2.5
Is IoT or sensor data being used to monitor production or equipment in real time?
Sustainability check
Pillar 03
Eco Factory
Capability to monitor, manage, and reduce environmental impact and resource use within production systems, including the ability to relate resource consumption to specific processes and operational decisions.
—
3.1
Is energy consumption measured at the process or machine level — or only at the total utility bill level?
3.2
Is waste and scrap quantified, categorised by type, and linked to specific production activities?
3.3
Are resource efficiency targets set, tracked with defined KPIs, and reviewed regularly?
3.4
Is environmental data used in operational decisions — not just reported after the fact?
3.5
Is there evidence of circular resource flows — reuse, recycling, or material recovery within or beyond the factory?
Sustainability check
Pillar 04
Customer-Focused Engineering
Capability to design and adapt products and processes based on customer requirements across the full product lifecycle, including consideration of environmental performance and end-of-life impact.
—
4.1
Are customer requirements formally captured and translated into product and process specifications?
4.2
Is there a structured feedback loop between customer use, quality outcomes, and product or process design?
4.3
Are lifecycle considerations (durability, repairability, end-of-life) part of the product design process?
4.4
How quickly can the factory adapt product design or specifications in response to a customer change request?
4.5
Has customer feedback driven measurable, documented improvements in the last 12 months?
Sustainability check
Pillar 05
Human-Centred Organisation
Capability to align workforce skills, roles, and organisational culture with evolving operational, technological, and sustainability requirements.
—
5.1
Are workers trained on current process standards, and is training updated when standards change?
5.2
Do floor-level workers use data or metrics to understand and manage their own area of work?
5.3
Is there a mechanism for workers to raise problems and suggest improvements — and is it actively used?
5.4
Can supervisors and middle managers lead data-driven problem-solving — not just relay instructions?
5.5
Is there evidence of workforce involvement in sustainability practices (energy awareness, waste reduction behaviour)?
Sustainability check
Pillar 06
Smart Manufacturing
Capability to use data and systematic analytical approaches — including, at higher maturity levels, AI-supported tools — to optimise operations and enable continuous improvement across multiple performance dimensions.
—
6.1
Are operational decisions based on structured data analysis — or primarily on experience and intuition?
6.2
Is there evidence of pattern recognition across multiple variables — linking process inputs to quality or efficiency outcomes?
6.3
Are predictive capabilities in use or actively being developed (predictive maintenance, demand forecasting)?
6.4
Are analytical tools — dashboards, SPC, AI-assisted tools — actively used by operations staff in daily work?
6.5
Can the factory run scenario analysis or simulation before implementing operational changes?
Sustainability check
Pillar 07
Value-Chain Open Factory
Capability to coordinate and integrate operations with suppliers, partners, and customers across the value chain, including collaboration on environmental and social sustainability performance.
—
7.1
Is information shared with key suppliers to support joint planning and coordination — beyond transactional ordering?
7.2
Can the factory receive and respond to customer demand signals faster than the standard order-acknowledgement cycle?
7.3
Is sustainability performance discussed or formally reported to supply chain partners?
7.4
Are inter-organisational data flows automated — or is coordination still primarily manual (phone, email, spreadsheet)?
7.5
Is the factory part of any platform, network, or ecosystem that connects it digitally to peers, partners, or customers?
Sustainability check
